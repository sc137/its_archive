Spring 2013 Technology Workshops

We carry them with us. We use them daily for our teaching, our work, our communication. The question is,  how do we integrate and balance our use of iPhones, iPads, Android Tablets, and other mobile devices for maximum benefit?

Our Technology Training Workshop series this semester will focus on three different components of this topic which are described in more detail below. Each session will be offered 3 times. Participants are welcome to attend 1 workshop or to return for further follow-up at subsequent sessions in that “stream.” Attendees are asked to bring their own mobile device(s).

Staff members are encouraged to discuss attendance at training sessions of interest with their supervisors and may receive release time with prior supervisor approval. Faculty will receive FLEX credit OR can apply for a Title V stipend if they intend to complete at least three workshops. Stipend applications are attached and must be submitted to Staff Development by Thursday, March 7th.

Workshop Stream 1: Mobile Audio and Video Recording

In this stream we will look at tools to help utilize your mobile device for audio and video recording. Topics covered may include

  • Youtube
  • Sound Cloud
  • Auphonic
  • Stand-alone applications
  • Editing recordings / Getting them from the device

Workshop Stream 2: Maximizing Professional and Personal Use of Your Mobile Device

We acknowledge that many of us are carrying powerful personal computers with us in the form of smart phones and tablet computers. We will attempt to find a balance between work and personal use in this series. This stream will discuss topics such as:

  • Segregating work files from personal files
  • Separating personal email and calendars from work
  • Off hours use - are you still "at work" when you aren't "supposed to be"?

Workshop Stream 3: Mobile Devices for the Classroom and Presentations

In this stream  we will look at ways of using our mobile devices for giving presentations and other classroom uses. Topics will be covered such as:

  • Connecting to a projector
  • Synchronizing speaking notes using Drop Box and Evernote

Banner Navigation Training

Banner Navigation Training is required for new users of Internet Native Banner and is available upon request. Please obtain and complete a Banner security form prior to scheduling training. Banner Navigation Training usually takes 1hr 30min to 2hr to complete the training and the testing.

You will receive your account shortly after successfully completing Banner Navigation Training. The security form will be submitted to the appropriate supervisor who assigns access privileges and then our support team will activate the account and notify you via e-mail.

Banner Navigation Training is not required for faculty using AccessRio.

SARS Software Products

We provide installation and usage support for SARS-GRID, SARS-TRAK, eSARS, and Counselor's Grid on campus. Please contact the help desk with your specific issue. Please indicate the computer name and location if you are requesting software installation.

Virtual Office Hours

IT Training will be holding virtual office hours throughout the semester using CCCConfer web-conferencing.

The idea behind virtual office hours is that we can have small groups and/or individuals pop-in for a specific question such as "how can I make my powerpoint file size smaller?" or "How do I archive my Outlook PST file?". Using CCCConfer for the virtual office hours allows part-time and off-campus staff members an easy way to utilize our training resources.

No virtual office hours are scheduled at this time.

Virtual Training

We provide over-the-phone and webinar style support for requested training using both CCCConfer and Team Viewer. Please feel free to contact us if you would like to schedule a virtual training.
