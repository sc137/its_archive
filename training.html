Spring 2014 Technology Workshops

Make Your Own Website at Wordpress.com

You want to create a blog or a full-fledged website. You want to share with your students, colleagues, community, friends, and the world. You want it to be free and easy.

This workshop will help you do that. We will create and customize a website using the free service from WordPress.com. In this workshop, you will learn how to:

  • Utilize the same software that will be used for the upcoming Rio web site
  • Create new websites or weblogs with customized looks
  • Update content by adding posts and pages
  • Install popular plugins to enhance and customize your new site

Why and How to Get Your Own Domain Name

Your domain name is your own piece of the internet landscape. You don’t have to be a company or organization to have your own domain name and it doesn’t cost very much. Owning your own domain name lets you control how you share with people on the internet.

In this workshop, we will discuss:

  • What is a gTLD and what does it mean? (.com, .net, .org, etc.)
  • What is domain privacy?
  • What is “hosting”? Do I need it?
  • What are the email options?
  • The value of having one location students reference for all of your online resources

Password Management with LastPass

Ten years ago you remembered all of your passwords. You had a system. It wasn’t perfect, but it worked.

That was three hundred websites ago. You are going to need some new passwords.

This workshop will walk you through the steps of using LastPass to manage your sensitive usernames and passwords. You can expect to discuss:

  • Is LastPass really secure?
  • Can I store passwords offline and online?
  • How do I generate a secure password?
  • How can I use LastPass to fill in forms for me?
  • How will using LastPass make my life easier?

Banner Navigation Training

Banner Navigation Training is required for new users of Internet Native Banner and is available upon request. Please obtain and complete a Banner security form prior to scheduling training. Banner Navigation Training usually takes 1hr 30min to 2hr to complete the training and the testing.

You will receive your account shortly after successfully completing Banner Navigation Training. The security form will be submitted to the appropriate supervisor who assigns access privileges and then our support team will activate the account and notify you via e-mail.

Banner Navigation Training is not required for faculty using AccessRio.

Virtual Office Hours

IT Training will be holding virtual office hours throughout the semester using CCCConfer web-conferencing.

The idea behind virtual office hours is that we can have small groups and/or individuals pop-in for a specific question such as "how can I make my powerpoint file size smaller?" or "How do I archive my Outlook PST file?". Using CCCConfer for the virtual office hours allows part-time and off-campus staff members an easy way to utilize our training resources.

No virtual office hours are scheduled at this time.
